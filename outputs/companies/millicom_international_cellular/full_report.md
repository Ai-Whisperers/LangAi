# Millicom International Cellular - Research Report

## Executive Summary

Millicom International Cellular S.A., operating under the **Tigo** brand, is a leading telecommunications and digital services provider focused exclusively on Latin American emerging markets. Founded in 1990 and headquartered in Luxembourg, the company has established itself as a dominant player in the region, serving over 46 million customers across nine markets as of December 2024. The company holds either the first or second position among operators in eight of its nine Latin American markets, with particularly strong leadership in Guatemala.

The company demonstrated robust financial performance in 2024, achieving record equity free cash flow of $777 million (exceeding its target) and reducing leverage to 2.42x. Full-year revenue reached $5.80 billion with 2.5% year-over-year growth, while Q4 2024 EBITDA hit $618 million, up 11% year-over-year. Millicom's business model centers on its "Triple A" strategy—making services Affordable, Accessible, and Available—positioning it well for continued growth in underserved emerging markets. The company targets approximately $750 million in equity free cash flow for 2025 and plans to distribute $500 million to shareholders while retaining funds for deleveraging and strategic initiatives.

## Key Insights

1. **Strong Financial Performance with Record Cash Generation:** Millicom achieved record equity free cash flow of $777 million in 2024, exceeding targets and demonstrating robust cash generation capabilities. The company reduced leverage to 2.42x while maintaining growth investments, positioning it well for both shareholder returns ($500 million distribution) and strategic initiatives.

The company's focus on underserved emerging markets with a population of nearly 300 million under mobile license provides significant long-term growth runway.

3. **Successful Converged Services Strategy:** The integration of mobile, fixed broadband, cable TV, and mobile financial services creates a comprehensive ecosystem that increases customer lifetime value and reduces churn. This converged approach differentiates Millicom from single-service competitors and addresses multiple needs of emerging market consumers.

4. **Strategic Expansion Through Telefónica Ecuador Acquisition:** The $380 million acquisition of Telefónica Ecuador strengthens Millicom's South American footprint and demonstrates management's commitment to strategic growth through selective M&A. This expansion aligns with the company's core competency in emerging market telecommunications.

5. **Triple "A" Strategy Drives Sustainable Growth:** Millicom's focus on making services Affordable, Accessible, and Available specifically targets the unique needs of emerging market consumers. This strategy, combined with mobile financial services addressing banking gaps, positions the company to capture growth as Latin American economies develop and digital adoption accelerates.
