# Tesla - Research Report

## Executive Summary

Tesla, Inc. is a vertically-integrated American manufacturer headquartered in Austin, Texas, that designs, develops, manufactures, and sells electric vehicles, energy generation and storage systems, and solar products globally. Founded as Tesla Motors to develop electric sports cars, the company has evolved into a multi-industry force under CEO Elon Musk's leadership, positioning itself as much as an AI and robotics company as an automotive manufacturer.

In 2024, Tesla generated $97.69 billion in total revenues, though the company faced significant profitability challenges with GAAP net income falling 53% to $7 billion from $15 billion in 2023 (adjusted for a one-time tax benefit). The automotive segment experienced headwinds with revenues declining 6% year-over-year, while the Energy Generation and Storage segment emerged as a bright spot with 67% revenue growth. The company maintains a strong market presence with 384,000 vehicle deliveries in Q2 2025 (up 29% year-over-year), extensive Supercharger infrastructure, and an expanding energy business that now contributes 15% of gross profit.

Despite financial pressures from aggressive pricing strategies and margin compression, Tesla continues to invest heavily in AI, autonomous driving technology, and manufacturing capacity, particularly in China. The company's lean executive structure and emphasis on vertical integration position it uniquely in the automotive and energy sectors, though leadership turnover and operational challenges present ongoing risks.

## Company Overview

**Legal Name:** Tesla, Inc.

**Headquarters:** Austin, Texas, United States

**Founding:** Tesla Motors was formed to develop electric sports cars, releasing its first vehicle, the completely electric Roadster, in 2008.

**Business Description:** Tesla operates as a vertically-integrated company across three primary segments:
- **Automotive:** Designs, manufactures, and sells electric sedans and SUVs through direct sales, operates Tesla Supercharger network, provides purchase financing and leasing, vehicle service through company-owned locations and mobile technicians, and offers vehicle warranties and extended service plans
- **Energy Generation and Storage:** Designs, manufactures, installs, and sells solar energy generation and energy storage products to residential, commercial, and industrial customers
- **Services and Other:** Provides vehicle servicing, repairs, and related services

**Key Leadership:**
- **Elon Musk** - CEO, oversees product development, engineering, and strategic direction across electric vehicles, energy storage, solar, AI, and robotics
- **Vaibhav Taneja** - CFO and Chief Accounting Officer, manages global financial operations, compliance, and reporting
- **Tom Zhu** - Senior Vice President of Manufacturing in China
- **Ashok Elluswamy** - Vice President of AI Software
- **Lars Moravy** - Vice President of Vehicle Engineering
- **Franz von Holzhausen** - Design Chief (with company since 2008)

**Governance:** ISS Governance QualityScore of 10 as of December 1, 2025 (decile rank relative to index)

## Key Insights

1. **Profitability Under Pressure:** Tesla's net income fell 53% in 2024 to $7 billion as aggressive pricing strategies to maintain market share compressed automotive margins from 23.3% to 18.4%, indicating the company is sacrificing short-term profitability for volume growth and market position in an increasingly competitive EV landscape.

2. **Energy Business Emerging as Critical Growth Driver:** The Energy Generation and Storage segment grew 67% to $10 billion in revenue with superior margins (26.2%) compared to automotive (18.4%), now contributing 15% of gross profit versus just 6% in 2023, suggesting Tesla's diversification strategy is creating a valuable second profit center.

3. **Delivery Growth Rebounding Strongly:** Q2 2025 deliveries of 384,000 units (up 29% year-over-year) demonstrate that despite 2024's revenue challenges, underlying demand for Tesla vehicles remains robust, particularly in international markets, validating the company's volume-focused strategy.

4. **Leadership Instability Poses Organizational Risk:** With two-thirds of Elon Musk's direct reports departing between 2021-2024, including complete turnover in HR leadership and multiple legal chiefs, Tesla faces significant organizational continuity challenges that could impact execution, though retention of core engineering leaders provides some stability.

5. **Strategic Pivot Toward AI and Robotics:** Tesla's explicit positioning as an AI and robotics company rather than purely an automaker, combined with heavy investment in autonomous driving technology and manufacturing automation, represents a fundamental business model evolution that could unlock new revenue streams but also introduces execution risk and requires sustained R&D investment during a period of margin pressure.
