## Top Conglomerates by Market Cap/Assets

### Mexico
1. **Grupo Carso** - $16.24B market cap (Carlos Slim)
2. **FEMSA** - $35B+ market cap (Coca-Cola bottling, OXXO)
3. **Grupo Bimbo** - $12B market cap (world's largest bakery)
4. **Cemex** - Global cement leader

### Brazil
1. **Itausa** - Controls Itau Unibanco (largest private bank in LATAM)
2. **J&F/JBS** - World's largest meat company
3. **Votorantim** - $71B+ assets under management
4. **Embraer** - 3rd largest aircraft manufacturer

### Chile
1. **Grupo Luksic (Quinenco)** - $71B+ AUM (mining, banking, shipping)
2. **Cencosud** - Largest retailer in Chile
3. **Falabella** - Department stores, Sodimac

### Colombia
1. **Grupo Empresarial Antioqueno** - ~5.5% of Colombia's GDP
2. **Grupo Aval** - Largest financial conglomerate
3. **Organizacion Ardila Lulle** - Beverages, media

### Peru
1. **Grupo Breca** - $4B+ family wealth, oldest conglomerate
2. **Grupo Romero** - $9B+ revenues, controls BCP bank
3. **Intercorp** - Fastest growing, $3.3B family wealth

## Research Notes

- All conglomerates are family-controlled (typical LATAM pattern)
- Many have 100+ year histories
- Cross-shareholding structures common (especially GEA in Colombia)
- Strong presence of immigrant-founded businesses (Italian, German, Lebanese)
- Recent trend: Gilinski breaking GEA structure in Colombia (2024)
- Corruption impacts: Odebrecht, Camargo Correa restructured post-Lava Jato
